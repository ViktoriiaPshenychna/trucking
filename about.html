An MC Number becomes essential when you're involved in inter-state commerce, which includes transporting goods across state lines. The MC Number serves as an identifier for carriers engaged in transporting regulated commodities for hire in inter-state commerce. It's a mandatory requirement set forth by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) and applies to goods altered from their natural state or recognized as regulated commodities.

NEW YORK PERMIT (NY) Required to enter New York with a gross vehicle weight of 18,000 pounds or higher.
OREGON PERMIT (OR) Required to enter Oregon with a gross vehicle weight of 18,000 pounds or higher •Also requires a $2,000 highway bond from insurance agency.
NEW MEXICO PERMIT (NM) Required to enter New Mexico with a gross vehicle weight of 26,000 pounds or higher.
KENTUCKY PERMIT (KYU) Required to enter Kentucky with a gross vehicle weight of 60,000 pounds or higher.

1. The vehicle must have three or more axles or have a gross vehicle weight or registered gross vehicle weight of more than 26,000 pounds and travel between at least two member jurisdictions.
2. The carrier's vehicles must travel in at least two member jurisdictions (U.S. states or Canadian provinces).
3. Carriers are required to keep detailed records of all fuel purchases and usage, including mileage records for each jurisdiction traveled.
4. Carriers need to designate a base jurisdiction in which they register and file their IFTA fuel tax returns.

The 2290 Heavy Highway Vehicle Use Tax Return is mandatory for all units weighing over 55,000 pounds, before purchasing IRP.
